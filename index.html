Charles Babbage

Charles Babbage was born on December 26, 1791, in London, England. He had two siblings, a brother named Henry and a sister name Mary. Charles Babbage was primarly educated at home until he went to Trinity College in 1810 and Peterhouse, Cambridge, in 1812 where he was the top mathematician. Not long after graduating, he was hired by the Royal Institution and got married. He had 8 kids and died on October 18, 1871, in London.



Accomplishments

In Charles Babbage's life, he accomplished and invented many things that contributed not only to computer science but to the entire world. One of Charles Babbage's contributions was helping founding the Analytical Society. Their goal was to introduce developments from European continent into English mathematics in 1812. Another one of his contribution which he is most famous for was the first automatic digital computer during the mid-1930s.
